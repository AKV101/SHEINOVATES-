Dummies Guide to Fantasy Football

Fantasy football is an app that takes real live stats from games and lets you draft your own "fantasy" team with any player from the NFL! By drafting players with higher stats you will get more points and to win in your league you want your team to do the best in their games and earn the most points! When you are drafting a team you can swap as many times as you want, but only before their game starts.

If you have never heard about fantasy football or always wanted to be in the conversation, but are not sure how to this is the place for you! We have created an algorithm that lets you draft your team before the offical draft on Fantasy Football to give you the highest chances of winning with limited knowledge about the game! We are using previous seasons stats for the players to predict their chances of suceeding this upcoming season.

You can draft a total of 15 players in your personalized fantasy football league! These 15 players fall into 2 categories active and bench. You will be able to pick 9 active players and 6 bench players who can replace your active players before each game starts. These players can come from any team and can be mixed to make your fantasy team!!


ACTIVE:

  • 1 Quaterback
  • 2 Running back's
  • 2 Wide receivers
  • 1 Flex player ( running back or wide receiver)
  • 1 Tight end
  • 1 defense
  • 1 kicker

BENCH:
  • 6 additional players of your choice in any of the positions!

Lets run through all the positions and what they do in the game! Each time a player moves or throws the ball they get a certain amount of points in a catagory! These categories include rushing yards, passing yards, touch downs, and interceptions!

  • Quaterback: starts of the game by making the plays and intiating the first move
    • hands of the ball to the running back (rushing yards)
    • passes the ball to a reveiver (passing yards)
    • run with the ball (rushing yards)
  • Running back: they are known to do it all!
    • lines up either behind or beside the quaterback
    • recieves handoffs from the quaterback to rush/ move the ball
    • block opponents offensive lineup
  • Wide receiver: the pass catchers
    • line up away from the main formation
    • main receivers of the quaterbacks throws
    • even though other players can catch the ball the wide recievers are the best at it!
  • Flex player (running back or wide receiver): look above!
  • Tight end: the best of both worlds
    • blocks for both the quaterbacks and running backs
    • runs into the field and catches passes
    • can catch like a receiver, but has the strength and size to defend
  • defense: the big and strong!
    • consists of 3 or 4 players who line up opposite with the other team
    • tackle ball carriers like the running back or wide recivers!
  • kicker: strong legs are key
    • responsible for kickoffs and field goals which happens after the touchdown!
